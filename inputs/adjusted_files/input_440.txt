Passive infrared (PIR) sensors are a common choice, detecting changes in infrared radiation caused by body heat. Ultrasonic sensors offer another approach, measuring the time it takes for sound waves to reflect off objects. They are less susceptible to temperature variations but can be less accurate in larger spaces. Furthermore, vision-based systems, utilizing cameras and image processing techniques, provide highly detailed occupancy information, but raise significant privacy concerns. The choice of technology depends critically on the specific application and the importance of factors like accuracy, cost, power consumption, and privacy. Ongoing research is focused on developing more sophisticated algorithms and sensor fusion techniques to overcome the limitations of individual sensor modalities. The integration of machine learning into occupancy detection systems is also a significant area of advancement, allowing for improved accuracy and adaptability to varying environmental conditions.
