Phase I trials typically involve a small group of healthy volunteers to assess the drug's safety and pharmacokinetics.  Phase II trials evaluate the drug's effectiveness in a larger group of patients with the target disease.  Phase III trials involve even larger, randomized, controlled studies to confirm the drug's efficacy and safety profile.  The rigorous design and regulatory oversight of clinical trials are essential for ensuring the safety and effectiveness of new medications.  However, the process is lengthy, expensive, and often faces challenges in recruiting and retaining participants.  Furthermore, the inclusion of diverse populations in clinical trials is paramount for ensuring that drugs are safe and effective for everyone.
