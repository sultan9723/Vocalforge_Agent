One significant hurdle is the complexity of fusing data from different modalities. Each modality presents unique challenges; for instance, aligning temporal information in audio and video streams requires sophisticated algorithms. Furthermore, the sheer volume of data required to train effective multimodal models presents a significant computational challenge, requiring powerful hardware and efficient training strategies. Another crucial area is ensuring data quality and consistency across modalities. Inconsistencies or biases in the training data can lead to flawed and unreliable models. The development of appropriate evaluation metrics is also a critical area of research.
