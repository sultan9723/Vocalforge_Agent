Real-time monitoring systems, coupled with advanced data analysis techniques, allow for the rapid identification of potential hazards and the implementation of timely interventions. The miniaturization of these technologies has also allowed for the development of personal radiation monitors, enabling individuals to track their own exposure levels. This predictive capacity is crucial for effective emergency response planning and mitigation strategies. However, these technological advancements also raise new challenges, including the need for skilled personnel to operate and maintain complex equipment, as well as the need for robust data security protocols to protect sensitive information. The integration of these technologies within existing infrastructure, such as power plants and hospitals, requires careful planning and significant investment.
