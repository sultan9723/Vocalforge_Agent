However, it's crucial to acknowledge the limitations of current AI technologies in biology.  The "black box" nature of some AI algorithms can make it difficult to understand the underlying mechanisms driving their predictions, raising concerns about the reliability and interpretability of results.  The need for high-quality, well-annotated data to train these algorithms is also a significant challenge.  Biased datasets can lead to biased algorithms, further highlighting the importance of careful data curation and validation.  Addressing these limitations is crucial for realizing the full potential of AI in revolutionizing biology and healthcare.
