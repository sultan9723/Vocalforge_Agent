This granular data allows utilities to better predict demand, optimize resource allocation, and proactively address potential grid issues.  Furthermore, AMI facilitates the integration of distributed energy resources (DERs), such as rooftop solar panels and energy storage systems, which are crucial for transitioning to a decentralized and decarbonized energy system.  The implementation of AMI, however, presents challenges.  The high initial cost of installation can be a significant barrier for some utilities, and ensuring data security and privacy is paramount.  Moreover, ensuring equitable access to AMI technology across all communities is crucial to prevent exacerbating existing inequalities in energy access.  The potential of AMI to improve grid efficiency and consumer engagement is significant, but careful planning and consideration of these challenges are essential for successful implementation.
