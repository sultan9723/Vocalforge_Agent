Speaker 2: Exactly. I'll further continue and I’d like to talk more about AI in legal tech, focusing specifically on its impact on access to justice.  The potential of AI to democratize legal services is significant.  AI-powered tools can offer affordable and accessible legal assistance to individuals who might otherwise be unable to afford traditional legal representation.  This is particularly relevant in areas like family law, landlord-tenant disputes, and consumer protection, where many individuals lack resources to navigate the complexities of the legal system.  However, the equitable distribution of these benefits is crucial.  The digital divide and lack of technological literacy could exacerbate existing inequalities, creating a two-tiered system where those with access to technology and digital skills benefit disproportionately.  Furthermore, the reliance on data-driven systems raises concerns about bias and fairness. If the training data reflects existing societal biases, the AI systems will perpetuate and even amplify these biases in their outputs. Addressing these challenges requires thoughtful policy interventions and the development of AI systems designed with fairness and inclusivity at their core.  Careful consideration of these factors is crucial to ensure that AI in legal tech truly serves the interests of justice for all.
